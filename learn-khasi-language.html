Learning Khasi language is easy and enjoyable, especially for beginners and travelers visiting Meghalaya. Khasi is written in the Roman script, which makes it simpler to read and pronounce compared to many other Indian languages. This guide will help you learn basic Khasi words, greetings, and daily-use phrases with clear meanings.

About the Khasi Language

Khasi is mainly spoken in the state of Meghalaya, especially in Shillong and the Khasi Hills. It is one of the few Austroasiatic languages spoken in India. Khasi uses Roman letters but has its own pronunciation rules, so learning the basics correctly is important.

Basic Khasi Greetings

Hello in Khasi

"Kumno" (pronounced: koom-no)

This is an informal way to say hello in Khasi.

Many travelers also use Khublei, which actually means thank you but is commonly used as a greeting.

How Are You?

Kumno phi long? (kum-no fee long)

This is an informal way to say hello in Khasi.

I am fine Nga bit nga biang ‚Äì I am well and fine

Congratulations! You have learned your first Khasi greetings.

Khasi Language and Gender Usage

In Khasi, even non-living objects have gender.

  • Ka sngi ‚Äì The sun (feminine)
